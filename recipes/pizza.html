Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 250g white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 150g active sourdough starter
  • 150ml luke-warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Sauce:

  • 1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 150g mozzarella cheese, thinly sliced
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Handful of fresh basil leaves (optional)

Optional Extras

  • Drizzle of balsamic glaze
  • Red chilli flakes
  • WHATEVER THE F*** YOU NEED TO ENJOY THIS AMAZING PEESHA

Step-by-step

Start the Dough

  1. Feed your starter: 8-12 hours before making the pizza, feed your sourdough starter with equal parts flour and water (approx. 50g each). Leave it at room temperature to become bubbly and active.
  2. Mix the dough: In a large bowl, combine the white bread flour, salt, and active sourdough starter. Gradually add the luke-warm water and olive oil, mixing until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough: Tip the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Add more flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  4. Bulk fermentation: Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp tea towel, and leave to rise at room temperature for 4-6 hours, performing stretch and folds every 30 minutes for the first 2 hours. (Stretch and fold: gently stretch the dough outwards, fold it over itself, and rotate the bowl. Repeat 4-5 times.)
  5. Shaping: After bulk fermentation, gently deflate the dough and shape it into a tight ball on a lightly floured surface. Place it seam-side down in a floured banneton (basket) or bowl, cover, and refrigerate for at least 12 hours (up to 36 hours).

Get the sauce going

  1. Simmer the sauce: In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

Assembly and Baking:

  1. Preheat oven and prepare: At least 30 minutes before baking, preheat your oven to its highest setting (ideally 240Â°C or higher). If using a baking stone, place it on the bottom rack to preheat. Otherwise, preheat a baking tray.
  2. Stretch the dough: On a lightly floured surface, gently stretch the chilled dough into a 12-inch circle, using your fingertips and knuckles. Don't worry about it being perfect!
  3. Transfer to baking surface: Carefully transfer the stretched dough to your baking stone or preheated tray dusted with semolina or cornmeal.
  4. Top the pizza: Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edge. Top with mozzarella cheese and drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Bake: Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly. Rotate the pizza halfway through for even baking.
  6. Finishing touches: Remove from the oven and top with fresh basil leaves and/or Parmesan cheese, if desired. Slice and enjoy!

Tips

  • For a crispier crust, preheat your baking stone or tray as hot as possible.
  • Don't overload the pizza with toppings - it can make the crust soggy.
  • Experiment with different flours for the dough, such as whole wheat or spelt, for added flavour and texture.
  • Use a pizza peel for easier transferring to and from the oven, if you have one.
  • Leftover pizza can be reheated in the oven or toaster oven for a quick and delicious snack.
